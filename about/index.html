My GnuPG Public Key

GNU Privacy Guard (a.k.a. GnuPG or GPG) is F/OSS that allows you to easily sign and encrypt your data. This enables you to verify people’s identities and protect your privacy when communicating online. If you would like to communicate securely with me, you will need to download my public key:

pub   1024D/50EDFCCD 2008-05-31
	  Key fingerprint = 7074 406E 8272 D083 1E31  2845 666F D4C1 50ED FCCD
	  uid                  Aaron Schaefer <aaron@elasticdog.com>
	  sub   2048g/388B7E9C 2008-05-31 [expires: 2010-08-28]

You can import it from any keyserver, or by downloading the ASCII armored version manually. DO NOT forget to verify the key against the fingerprint listed above to ensure that they are the same. For more information, see The GNU Privacy Handbook.

About This Site

ElasticDog is the place where I house all of my musings on technology, standards-based web design, personal finance, the arts, programming, life, pirates…whatever strikes my fancy. I tend to write a lot of “howto” articles on various technical subjects, usually stemming from when I have to research something complicated and don’t want to forget how I did it. If my writing can also help other people, then all the better! Participation is always welcome.
